# Compensation Policy

## Purpose

This policy establishes TalentLens's standards for competitive, equitable, and transparent compensation practices. It defines how salary bands are set, when pay equity reviews are triggered, and how peer-pay gap thresholds are measured and remediated. All compensation decisions must comply with applicable wage laws and support the company's commitment to pay equity across gender, ethnicity, and protected class status.

## Compensation Philosophy

TalentLens targets the **60th percentile** of the relevant market for base compensation, with total compensation (base + bonus + equity) targeted at the **75th percentile** for roles in high-demand technical disciplines. Market data is refreshed annually using Radford and Mercer survey data, cross-referenced against regional cost-of-labor indices.

## Peer-Pay Gap Thresholds and Equity Reviews

The TalentLens platform continuously monitors each employee's base salary against their **peer cohort median** — defined as employees at the same job level, in the same function, with ±2 years of tenure, in the same geographic region.

**Automatic pay equity review is triggered when:**
- An employee's base salary falls **more than 15% below** their peer cohort median (compa-ratio < 0.85).
- An employee has received no merit increase in the prior **24 months** and their peer cohort median has increased by more than 8%.
- A new hire in the same role and level is offered a salary within **10% above** an existing employee's current pay, creating internal compression.

When a review trigger fires, the Compensation team has **30 days** to complete a formal equity analysis and present findings to the relevant HR Business Partner and Finance team. If a pay gap is confirmed, remediation must be implemented within the next pay cycle or the following quarter, whichever is sooner.

## Pay Equity Review Procedures

1. **Trigger identification**: Automated flag from TalentLens attrition risk model or manual submission by HR BP.
2. **Cohort construction**: HR Analytics defines the comparison group using the criteria above.
3. **Gap quantification**: Calculate the dollar and percentage gap between the employee's current salary and the cohort median.
4. **Root cause analysis**: Determine whether the gap is attributable to performance differentiation, tenure, geographic adjustment, or unexplained variance.
5. **Remediation options**: Off-cycle merit increase, reclassification to a higher level, retention bonus (if immediate flight risk), or documented justification if no action is warranted.
6. **Audit trail**: All findings and actions are recorded in the compensation audit log, accessible to HR leadership and Legal.

## Annual Merit Planning

Merit increases are distributed during the Q1 planning cycle (February effective date). Standard merit pool is 3.5% of payroll. Employees flagged as Critical attrition risk are eligible for an additional out-of-cycle retention adjustment up to 10% of base salary, subject to manager and VP approval.

## Confidentiality

All individual compensation data is strictly confidential. Sharing salary information outside of approved HR and Finance channels is a policy violation.
