# Performance Management Policy

## Purpose and Scope

This policy outlines TalentLens's approach to employee performance management, including review cadences, rating calibration, Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) procedures, and manager escalation paths for employees who are consistently performing below expectations. The goal is to ensure fair, transparent, and documented performance feedback that supports both employee development and organizational effectiveness.

## Performance Review Cadence

TalentLens follows a **bi-annual formal review cycle**:

- **Mid-Year Check-In (July)**: Informal progress review against goals set in January. No formal rating assigned. Managers document key observations in the HRIS. Employees flagged as attrition risk receive a mandatory stay conversation during this cycle.
- **Annual Performance Review (December)**: Full calibration cycle with ratings, goal attainment scores, and compensation recommendations. Ratings are calibrated across peer groups to prevent rating inflation.

In addition to formal cycles, managers are expected to conduct **monthly one-on-ones** and document at least one substantive piece of feedback per quarter in the HRIS performance module.

## Performance Rating Scale

| Rating | Label | Definition |
|--------|-------|-----------|
| 5 | Exceptional | Consistently exceeds all goals; demonstrable organizational impact |
| 4 | Exceeds Expectations | Meets all goals and exceeds several; strong peer feedback |
| 3 | Meets Expectations | Achieves core goals; solid, reliable contributor |
| 2 | Partially Meets | Meets some but not all goals; improvement required in key areas |
| 1 | Does Not Meet | Fails to meet core goals; formal intervention required |

Rating distributions are calibrated so that no more than 10% of a team receives a 5, and Partially Meets + Does Not Meet together should not exceed 15% in a healthy team without documented extenuating circumstances.

## Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) Procedures

A PIP is initiated when an employee receives a rating of **1 (Does Not Meet)** in any formal review cycle, or when a manager documents three consecutive months of material performance deficiencies in the HRIS.

### PIP initiation steps:

1. **Manager-HR BP alignment meeting**: Before a PIP is communicated to the employee, the manager must consult with the assigned HR Business Partner to confirm the decision is supported by documented evidence.
2. **PIP document preparation**: HR BP drafts the PIP in collaboration with the manager. The document must include: specific performance gaps with measurable examples, clear success criteria for each gap, support resources (training, coaching, mentorship), and a defined review timeline (standard: 30, 60, or 90 days depending on role complexity).
3. **Employee meeting**: The manager and HR BP present the PIP to the employee. The employee must sign acknowledging receipt (signature does not imply agreement).
4. **Progress check-ins**: Weekly documented check-ins between manager and employee throughout the PIP period.
5. **PIP outcome**: At the end of the PIP period, one of three outcomes is documented: (a) PIP closed — performance standards met, (b) PIP extended — partial progress, additional 30 days, or (c) PIP failed — separation process initiated per the offboarding policy.

## Manager Escalation Paths for Low Performers

When a manager identifies sustained underperformance, the escalation path is:

1. **Level 1 — Manager coaching**: Informal verbal feedback and documented HRIS notes. No HR involvement required for first 60 days.
2. **Level 2 — HR BP consultation**: If coaching does not produce improvement within 60 days, manager consults HR BP. A written performance warning may be issued.
3. **Level 3 — Formal PIP**: Initiated per the procedures above if the written warning does not result in improvement within 30 days.
4. **Level 4 — VP/HR Director notification**: If a PIP fails or if the employee's role is at L4 or above, the VP and HR Director are notified before any separation decision is made.
5. **Level 5 — Legal review**: Required for all separations involving employees in protected classes, employees on FMLA/leave, or those who have filed an HR complaint in the prior 12 months.

## Intersection with Attrition Risk Model

Employees with a performance rating of 1 or 2 who simultaneously carry a High or Critical attrition risk score are flagged for dual-track intervention: the manager must address both performance deficits and flight risk drivers in parallel. The attrition risk model's SHAP features are shared with the HR BP to inform whether the performance issue may be a symptom of a deeper engagement problem (e.g., pay inequity, poor manager relationship, lack of growth opportunities) that should be addressed alongside the performance conversation.
